I write under the name MellemMøderne — "between the meetings" — because that's where most of my thinking actually happens.

For over a decade I worked as a management consultant, mostly in rooms where the loudest, fastest opinion won. I got good at that game. Then I noticed the leaders I respected most weren't playing it — they were listening, deciding slowly, and somehow still moving faster than everyone else.

Now I coach individual leaders and teams who want to work that way: fewer, better meetings; feedback that lands; decisions that hold. I bring the same discipline I write about publicly — no frameworks for their own sake, no performance of confidence. Just what's actually working.

What I offer

Two ways to work together

Both start the same way — a conversation about what's actually happening, not what should be.

01

Individual coaching

For leaders — often introverted, often senior — who've been told to be more visible and suspect that's the wrong advice.

  • Bi-weekly sessions, 3–6 months
  • Built around real decisions and meetings, not theory
  • Async written reflection between sessions
02

Team development

For teams whose meeting culture has quietly stopped working — where the same three people talk and everyone else edits themselves.

  • Diagnostic conversations with the full team
  • A small number of structural changes, not a program
  • Follow-up check-in at 90 days
